Hey there!

I'm Marco. A Telecommunications Ingeneer who found the dev world more interesting than waves and antennas.

After completing my degree, I decided to study a Master of Web Development. Even when it was quite interesting and I aquired useful knowledge, I still wasn't feeling at ease with what I was doing. Everything was... too theorical.

I was looking forward to doing 'useful things'. I wanted to apply what I had learnt, and to deal with companies problems. So, before finishing my educational stage, I started working as a developer.

It was then, when I finally got to work in real projects, when I really decided to turn my hobby into a proffesion.

I've always liked the pragmatic approach of problems. This attribute makes me very fond of creating software that fulfull necessities. Not just something theoretical, but something people can use and be proud of.

This objetive, however, has not a direct and unique path. Software development -as many other aspects in live- should not only be focused on the final goal; on answering the question: does it work? It should also take care of how it is done. Is it legible? Is it scalable? Does it perform as it should? Does it follow recommended good practises?

Being curoius and nonconformist made me understand the importance of the journey to reach a destination, not only the final product. These qualities, among others, are what make a person improve everyday, every line of code.

And so, does the destination matter? Or is it the path we take? I declare that no accomplishment has substance nearly as great as the road used to achieve it. We are not creatures of destinations. It is the journey that shapes us. Our callused feet, our backs strong from carrying the weight of our travels, our eyes open with the fresh delight of experiences lived.

Feel free to contact me if you need something. Surely, I will be doing better coding than when writing these lines ;)

And last, but not least, a quote I used to afirm not so many years ago. Nowadays, I do think it was a good start.

Programming is a hobby, but it will never be my job
